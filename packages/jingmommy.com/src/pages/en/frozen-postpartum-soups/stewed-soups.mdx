### Mild-Warming Tonic Soups

Traditional Chinese medicine recognizes that the early postpartum period is characterized by "deficiency of both qi and blood." At this stage, gentle nourishment is essential — supporting blood production, strengthening the spleen and stomach, and allowing the body to stabilize and heal before moving to more intensive supplementation. Mild-warming tonic soups are mellow and non-drying; they nourish qi and blood without burdening the body. Suitable for early postpartum, post-surgical recovery, a weakened constitution, or general daily wellness.

---

### Intensive Tonic Soups

As qi and blood gradually recover, the focus shifts to "tonifying the kidneys, warming the uterus, and consolidating the foundation" — intensifying warmth, replenishing vitality, and strengthening the body's core. Intensive tonic soups feature warming, qi- and blood-nourishing ingredients and herbs. Suitable for the later postpartum period, those with a cold constitution, or winter supplementation.

Phased nourishment lets the body return to its best state gradually and naturally — no rushing, restoring vitality from the inside out.  
Completing a full month of postpartum recovery in America is no easy feat. Cooking at home takes time and energy; there are simply no low-sodium, lightly seasoned, confinement-appropriate soups available outside. Jingmommy uses central kitchen professional equipment and slow-simmering techniques to freeze our postpartum tonic soups with their flavors fully intact — real, wholesome ingredients, just open and heat to enjoy.  
Convenient for yourself, impressive as a gift: whether for postpartum recovery, post-surgical healing, or winter nourishment, you can follow the natural progression and restore your foundation step by step.
