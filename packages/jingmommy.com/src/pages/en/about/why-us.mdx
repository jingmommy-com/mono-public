Jingmommy is a 100% self-operated central kitchen postpartum meal delivery service. Unlike typical home kitchens, a government-licensed central kitchen must undergo unannounced high-standard health inspections by the health department and follow a rigorous food-handling monitoring process — serving as the first line of defense for moms. The central kitchen also represents Jingmommy's commitment to quality: only a self-operated central kitchen can stand behind its meals and protect clients' interests.

Some operators claim to have a central kitchen but never open it for visits (only showing photos), because it is actually another restaurant's kitchen — not their own. These so-called "central kitchens" cook postpartum meals in the morning and run other businesses the rest of the time. These operators cannot provide product liability insurance (they don't have a restaurant license), and even if they have general business insurance, it does not guarantee the quality, hygiene, or safety of the food. Worse still, this is not legal — health regulations require that a kitchen facility be operated by only one business and cannot be shared.

![about_29](@/assets/about_29.png)

The central kitchen also represents Jingmommy's commitment to culinary professionalism. We are willing to invest in owning our own central kitchen rather than settling for rented space or outsourcing production. Jingmommy's central kitchen is the only one in Los Angeles that welcomes visits and open tastings — all of which reflects our strict quality standards and commitment to protecting our clients.

**Infant First Aid Class:**
![about_38](@/assets/about_38.png)

According to the U.S. National Safety Council, accidental death and disability in young children exceeds all diseases combined. The target is to perform Basic Life Support within 4 minutes and Advanced Cardiac Life Support within 8 minutes to save lives in critical situations. Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR) is a combination of external chest compressions and mouth-to-mouth resuscitation to maintain heartbeat and breathing. This course focuses on pediatric basic life support for infants under one year of age and young children. All instruction and practice follows American Heart Association (AHA) curriculum. Training time is two and a half hours, including hands-on practice and written and practical exams. The course is taught by an American Heart Association Pediatric First Aid certified / CPR licensed instructor.

**Baby Massage Class:**
![about_39](@/assets/about_39.png)

Baby massage is an ancient form of human wisdom and a long-standing therapeutic practice. During natural birth, the fetus already receives a special massage through the pressure of the mother's birth canal. Baby massage is also a form of "touch therapy" — a way for parents to bond with their baby through skin-to-skin contact. The skin is the body's largest sensory organ, the earliest sense to develop, and the primary way infants receive information from the world. Extensive clinical research confirms that parental massage provides numerous developmental benefits for babies and is widely practiced worldwide. A gentle massage for your baby is not merely physical touch — it is also a transmission of love. The class is taught by a Certified Infant Massage Educator.
